(`internal/eventbus/authguard/authguard.go:105`) and declares exactly the two methods
`*plugins.Manager` already implements with identical signatures
(`internal/plugin/manager.go:1749`, `:1768`). The cut is pure dependency inversion: delete the
adapter, let structural satisfaction do the work. Verified live this session.

**But the adapter carried a nil-guard that structural satisfaction does not.** The deleted
`manifestAdapter` returned `false` when its wrapped manager was nil
(`adapter_manifest.go:18-20`, `:25-27`). A typed-nil `*plugins.Manager` stored in a

receiver would reach `m.lookupManifest` → `m.mu.RLock()` → panic. The guard must move onto the
two `Manager` methods. This is the load-bearing detail of seam 2 and is why Task 2 is a `tdd`
task rather than a mechanical delete.

"does not exist" note in `.claude/rules/references/plan-review-learnings.md` is stale; it is

eventbus subpackages (`cryptowiring.go:20-22`), which looks like a third seam while you are already

already one-way. Noted, not touched. Do not add it to this plan's scope or to the Wave C ratchet's
forbidden-edge table.

**Seam 3 does not exist (retracted).** CONTEXT.md's original D-09 listed a `TranslateSubscribeErr`
